## Research Limitations & Methodological Constraints

### Technical Limitations

- **Image Quality Dependency**: Analysis accuracy is directly dependent on satellite image resolution, atmospheric conditions, and sensor calibration. Poor quality images may lead to reduced vegetation detection accuracy.

- **NDVI Approximation**: This study approximates the Normalized Difference Vegetation Index (NDVI) using RGB spectral channels instead of true near-infrared (NIR) data. While HSV-based vegetation detection provides reasonable estimates, it cannot fully replicate the spectral precision of dedicated vegetation indices.

- **Temporal Resolution Constraints**: Analysis is limited to available image timestamps and may not capture important seasonal variations, phenological cycles, or short-term environmental events that occur between measurement periods.

### Model and Dataset Constraints

- **Training Dataset Limitations**: The classification model is trained on the limited EuroSAT dataset, which primarily represents European land cover patterns. Generalization to other geographic regions, climate zones, or land use patterns may be limited.

- **Linear Trend Assumption**: The prediction model assumes linear temporal trends in vegetation change. This may not adequately capture complex environmental dynamics, non-linear responses to climate change, or threshold effects in ecosystem behavior.

- **Patch-based Analysis Constraints**: The sliding window approach analyzes discrete image patches, which may miss fine-scale spatial heterogeneity, edge effects, or landscape connectivity patterns that are important for ecological assessment.

### Methodological Boundaries

- **Spatial Scale Limitations**: Analysis is constrained to the spatial resolution and extent of input images. Sub-pixel vegetation patterns and landscape-scale processes may not be fully captured.

- **Validation Constraints**: Model validation is based on statistical metrics and temporal consistency. Ground-truth validation with field measurements would strengthen confidence in results.

- **Uncertainty Quantification**: While confidence assessments are provided, full uncertainty propagation through the analysis chain is not implemented.

### Recommendations for Future Research

- Incorporate true multispectral or hyperspectral imagery with NIR bands for accurate NDVI calculation
- Expand training datasets to include diverse geographic regions and land cover types
- Implement non-linear prediction models to capture complex environmental dynamics
- Conduct field validation studies to verify remote sensing-based vegetation assessments
- Develop uncertainty quantification frameworks for comprehensive error analysis
