## 📊 Research-Level Terminology Guidelines

### **Academic Language Principles**
1. **Use "observed" instead of "shows"**
2. **Use "documented" instead of "detected"**
3. **Use "vegetation coverage" instead of "greenery"**
4. **Use "temporal" instead of "time-based"**
5. **Use "systematic" instead of "consistent"**
6. **Use "anthropogenic" instead of "human"**
7. **Use "substantial" instead of "major"**
8. **Use "comprehensive" instead of "complete"**

### **Professional Descriptors**
- **"Significant increase in vegetation coverage observed"**
- **"Systematic reduction in green space documented"**
- **"Comprehensive temporal analysis indicates..."**
- **"Substantial landscape modification detected"**
- **"Inter-annual fluctuations demonstrate..."**
- **"Predictive model indicates..."**
- **"Environmental assessment reveals..."**
- **"Observational data suggests..."**

### **Scientific Precision**
- **Quantified statements**: "1.6% annually" instead of "per year"
- **Technical terms**: "Inter-annual" instead of "year-to-year"
- **Formal language**: "Exhibits" instead of "shows"
- **Academic tone**: "Assessment" instead of "check"
- **Research context**: "Study period" instead of "time frame"
